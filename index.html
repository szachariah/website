We compete in the annually held NASA Robotic Mining Competition at the NASA Kennedy Space Center located in Cape Canaveral, FL. NASA's mission is to allow college students to find innovative mining techniques to obtain the water-ice found under the surface of Mars. This in turn will someday allow humans to "live off the land" or in scientific terms "In Situ Resource Utilization (ISRU)". My team's objective every year is to build a martian rover from scratch, find new innovative mining techniques, and also develop software to make our robot navigate and dig autonomously. Last year, being a first-year organization, we were able to secure third place in Mining beating out forty-nine other universities which include some top tier schools such University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign, Purdue University, University of Michigan at Ann Arbor, New York University, etc. Other than my administrative roles of overseeing the organization, I worked within the Electrical Team where I was able to assist in designing the power systems of the robot.
